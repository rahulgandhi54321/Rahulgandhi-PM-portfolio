01 Start with Why

Start with
Why.

"People don't buy what you do —
they buy why you do it."

— Simon Sinek · A principle I live by

The gap between a brilliant idea and a successful product is one PM — someone who starts with the customer, owns the business outcome, and ships with precision. That's the role I was built for. Every decision I've made has been anchored in one question: does this move the metric that matters to the customer?

◎

Customer Obsession

Every decision starts with jobs-to-be-done. Engineering constraints and business targets exist to serve the customer — not the other way around.

◈

Outcomes Over Output

Features are a means. The end is always a measurable change — in behaviour, retention, or revenue. $100M+ GMV happened by tracking the right metrics.

◉

AI-First Thinking

AI isn't a feature I add. It's the lens through which I see every product problem — from LLM-powered analytics to ML-driven propensity models.

◐

First Principles

Never assume. Never copy. Start from what is true, build from there. The best products ignored what came before and asked: what should this actually be?
